Basic Phonics Unit

Welcome to the Basic Phonics Unit! Progress through the stages below to enhance your understanding and skills in phonics, focusing on different word structures.

Stage 1: CVC (Consonant-Vowel-Consonant)

Start with the basics of phonics by learning about CVC words, which are formed by a consonant, followed by a vowel, and ending with another consonant. These are foundational for beginning readers.

Begin Stage 1

Stage 2: CCVC (Consonant-Consonant-Vowel-Consonant)

Advance to more complex structures with CCVC words, where two consonants precede the vowel, providing a challenge in blending sounds together.

Begin Stage 2

Stage 3: CVCC (Consonant-Vowel-Consonant-Consonant)

Explore CVCC word structures, focusing on ending blends after a single vowel. This stage helps refine decoding skills and sound segmentation.

Begin Stage 3

Stage 4: CCVCC (Consonant-Consonant-Vowel-Consonant-Consonant)

Master phonics with CCVCC words, integrating both beginning and ending blends around a central vowel. This stage focuses on more complex phonetic arrangements and spelling patterns.

Begin Stage 4
